Destroy
Notifies C++ that the movie is going to be destroyed, so it's removed from the rendering queue and will not be advanced anymore. The actual destruction of the movie object will take place when the movie destructor is called. Ensure that there are no C# references to a movie that you want to be released, otherwise the destructor will not be called and the movie will still stick around even though it won't be advanced/displayed.
(Inherited from Movie.)
